Monkeys are divided into two main groups: Old World monkeys (Africa and Asia) and New World monkeys (Central and South America)
Capuchin monkeys are known for using tools - they use stones to crack nuts and sticks to extract insects
Spider monkeys have no thumbs but incredibly long arms that help them swing through trees at speeds up to 35 mph
Howler monkeys are the loudest land animals - their calls can be heard up to 3 miles away through dense rainforest
Japanese macaques (snow monkeys) are the only primates besides humans that live in snowy climates and enjoy hot spring baths
Proboscis monkeys have huge noses that amplify their calls and help attract mates - the bigger the nose, the more attractive to females
Mandrill monkeys are the largest primates in the world and males have colorful blue and red faces that get brighter when excited
Vervet monkeys have different alarm calls for different predators - eagles, leopards, and snakes each get their own warning sound
Golden lion tamarins have luxurious manes and were once nearly extinct but conservation efforts brought them back from just 200 individuals
Bonobos share 98.8% of their DNA with humans and are known for solving conflicts through social bonding rather than aggression
